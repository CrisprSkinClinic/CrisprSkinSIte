If you've looked into acne scar treatment before, you've probably seen a long list of available procedures without much explanation of which one is actually right for your specific scarring. That's because the answer genuinely depends on which type of scar you have.

## Three Distinct Patterns

Icepick scars are narrow and deep, almost like a small puncture. Boxcar scars are broader, with sharply defined edges, giving the skin a pitted appearance. Rolling scars are broad too, but with sloping, less-defined edges that create a wave-like unevenness rather than sharp pits.

Most people have a mixture of these rather than one uniform type, which is part of why a proper evaluation matters more here than in almost any other cosmetic dermatology treatment.

## Why One Treatment Doesn't Fit All Three

Microneedling, which stimulates the skin's own collagen production, tends to work well for rolling and boxcar scars but does less for the sharply-defined depth of icepick scars. Chemical peels help with overall texture and superficial scarring but have limits with deeper scarring of any type. Matching the treatment to the actual scar pattern present is what determines whether a course of treatment produces meaningful improvement.

## Setting Honest Expectations

We're upfront that acne scar treatments improve the appearance and texture of scarring, often meaningfully, but complete removal isn't a realistic promise for established scarring. What's achievable depends on your specific scar type and depth, which is exactly why classifying your scarring correctly at the start of treatment matters so much.
