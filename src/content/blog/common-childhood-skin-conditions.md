Parents bring children in for a wide range of skin concerns, but a handful of conditions make up the large majority of what we see. Here's a quick guide to the most common ones, and roughly what to expect.

## Atopic Dermatitis (Eczema)

The most common chronic skin condition in children, presenting as dry, itchy, inflamed patches -- often on the face and scalp in infants, and in the creases of elbows and knees in older children. Management centers on a consistent moisturizing routine and appropriate treatment for flares, and many children see meaningful improvement as they grow.

## Molluscum Contagiosum

Small, smooth, pearly bumps caused by a common viral infection. It's contagious but generally harmless, and often resolves on its own within months to about a year -- meaning watchful waiting is frequently the most appropriate approach rather than immediate treatment.

## Birthmarks

Most birthmarks, whether vascular or pigmented, are entirely benign, and many either fade over time or simply remain a stable, harmless part of a child's skin. An evaluation early on provides accurate reassurance about what type your child has and its expected course.

## Diaper Dermatitis

Simple irritation from prolonged moisture contact, generally responding well to frequent diaper changes, gentle cleansing, and a barrier cream. Rash that's more severe or not improving with basic care can involve a secondary infection needing separate treatment.

## When to Bring Your Child In

Any persistent rash, unusual growth, or skin change that isn't improving with basic home care is worth an evaluation -- particularly if it's affecting your child's comfort, sleep, or daily activities. We aim to make these visits as comfortable as possible for children, working at their pace and explaining things in an age-appropriate way.
