We deliberately use the term "reduction" rather than "removal" when talking about laser hair treatment, and it's worth explaining why that distinction matters for setting the right expectations from the start.

## Why a Single Session Isn't Enough

Hair grows in cycles, and at any given time, only a portion of the hair in a treated area is in its active growth phase -- the phase laser treatment actually affects. This means a single session only impacts some of the hair present. A full course of multiple sessions, spaced to catch hair as it cycles through this active phase, is what produces meaningful, cumulative reduction.

## Skin and Hair Type Affect Results

Laser hair reduction works best on darker, coarser hair against lighter skin, since the laser targets pigment in the hair follicle. It's less effective on very light, fine, or grey hair, regardless of the equipment or provider -- this is a function of the technology itself, not something to shop around for a workaround to.

## What "Reduction" Actually Looks Like

Most patients see a substantial, lasting reduction in hair growth after a full course of treatment, with any regrowth typically finer and sparser than before. This is a genuinely good outcome for most people, but it's different from a claim of permanent, complete removal for every patient -- some regrowth over time, and occasional maintenance sessions, are normal parts of the picture.

## Setting Up Realistic Expectations From the Start

At your consultation, we assess your specific skin and hair type and lay out a realistic session count and expected outcome based on that assessment -- not a generic number quoted before we've actually looked at what we're working with.
