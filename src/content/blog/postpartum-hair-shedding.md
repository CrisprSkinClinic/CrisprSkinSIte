If you've had a baby in the last few months and are suddenly noticing a lot more hair coming out in the shower or brush, you're not imagining it, and in most cases, this isn't a sign that something has gone wrong.

## Why It Happens

During pregnancy, elevated hormone levels keep more hair than usual in its active growth phase, which is why many women notice thicker, fuller hair while pregnant. After delivery, hormone levels drop sharply, and that extra hair shifts into its resting phase together, shedding over the following months rather than gradually as it normally would.

## The Typical Timeline

Shedding usually starts around two to three months after delivery, peaks somewhere in that window, and then gradually tapers off. Most women see this settle back to normal within six to twelve months. It can feel alarming in the moment, especially if you're already sleep-deprived and adjusting to a new baby, but it is a well-recognized, temporary pattern.

## When It's Worth a Visit

If shedding continues well beyond a year, or if you're noticing thinning that seems disproportionate or isn't following this typical improving pattern, it's worth an evaluation. Iron deficiency and thyroid changes are both common in the postpartum period and can compound normal postpartum shedding -- these are identifiable and very treatable when caught.

## The Reassurance That Actually Helps

For most new mothers we see with this concern, the single most helpful thing we can offer is accurate information: this is expected, it has a typical timeline, and it resolves on its own for the large majority of women. We're also happy to check the less common contributing factors if your pattern doesn't match this typical course, or if the reassurance alone doesn't put your mind at ease.
