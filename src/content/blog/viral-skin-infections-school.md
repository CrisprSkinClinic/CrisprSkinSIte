Molluscum contagiosum and viral warts are two of the most common reasons children come in for a dermatology visit, and one of the first questions parents ask is whether their child needs to stay home from school. In most cases, the answer is no.

## What These Infections Actually Are

Both are viral skin infections -- molluscum presenting as small, smooth, pearly bumps, and warts as rougher-textured growths -- spreading through direct skin contact or shared items. They're common enough that they often appear in small clusters among children in close contact, like classmates or siblings.

## Treatment Is Often Optional, Not Mandatory

Many of these infections resolve on their own over months to about a year without any treatment at all. Because of this, watchful waiting -- simply monitoring while following basic hygiene guidance -- is frequently the most appropriate approach, and we're always clear that this is a legitimate medical choice, not a lack of care on our part or yours.

## When Active Treatment Makes Sense

If the lesions are bothering your child, spreading significantly, or you'd simply prefer not to wait, gentle in-clinic removal methods appropriate for children are available. This is a reasonable choice too -- it's a matter of preference once you understand the trade-offs, not a medical necessity in most cases.

## Practical School and Daily Life Guidance

Covering visible lesions and encouraging your child not to scratch or pick at them helps limit spread to classmates without requiring time away from school. We're happy to provide a note or guidance letter if your child's school requests documentation.

## The Reassurance Parents Usually Need

These infections are common, generally harmless, and self-limiting for most children. An evaluation confirms the diagnosis and gives you a clear, practical plan -- whether that's watching and waiting or opting for active treatment -- so you're not left guessing.
