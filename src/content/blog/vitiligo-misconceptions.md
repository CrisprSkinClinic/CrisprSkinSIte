Vitiligo is surrounded by more misinformation than almost any other common skin condition we treat, much of it rooted in old misconceptions rather than current medical understanding. It's worth addressing some of these directly.

## It Is Not Contagious

Vitiligo cannot be spread through contact with someone who has it. It's an autoimmune condition -- the immune system mistakenly attacks the cells responsible for skin pigment -- with no infectious component whatsoever.

## It's Not Caused by Diet Alone

Despite persistent folk beliefs in some communities, vitiligo has no established connection to diet as a root cause. It's a genuine autoimmune process, and no dietary change reliably prevents or reverses it on its own.

## There's Currently No Guaranteed Cure -- and We Say So Directly

We think patients deserve honesty here: current treatment can meaningfully restore pigment in many cases, especially with early and consistent treatment, but there is no guaranteed cure, and results vary by body area and how active the condition currently is. We won't promise outcomes we can't reliably deliver, even though we understand that's not always what people hope to hear.

## The Course Genuinely Varies Between People

Some patients have a few small, stable patches for years. Others experience more extensive or actively spreading depigmentation. This variability is real and not something we can predict with certainty at a first visit -- which is part of why ongoing evaluation and honest communication matter throughout treatment, not just at the start.

## What Actually Helps

Treatment response varies by body area -- the face tends to respond best, hands and feet the least reliably -- and by how long a patch has been present. For patients who want it, cosmetic camouflage is also a legitimate option alongside medical treatment, not instead of it.
