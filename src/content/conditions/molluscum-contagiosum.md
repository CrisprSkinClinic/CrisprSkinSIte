Molluscum contagiosum is a common viral skin infection in children, presenting as small, smooth, pearly bumps, often with a characteristic central dimple. It spreads through direct skin contact or shared items, which is why it often appears in small clusters among children in close contact with one another.

Because molluscum commonly resolves on its own within months to about a year without treatment, watchful waiting -- combined with simple hygiene guidance to limit spread -- is often the most appropriate approach, and this is explained clearly to parents as a legitimate medical choice rather than a lack of care. Active removal remains an option where the child is bothered by the lesions or a parent prefers it.
