Chemical peels use a controlled chemical solution to remove the outer layers of skin, encouraging renewal and improving texture, tone, and clarity. They range from very superficial peels with essentially no downtime to deeper peels that require a short recovery period, and the right choice depends on your skin type, your specific concern, and how much downtime you're able to accommodate.

Peel selection matters more than people often expect -- the wrong peel type, or the wrong strength for your skin type, can worsen pigmentation rather than improve it, particularly in melasma-prone or naturally darker skin. This is a big part of why we recommend a dermatologist-guided evaluation rather than choosing a peel based on marketing alone.

## What to Expect at Your Visit

We assess your skin type, current concerns, and any relevant history -- including prior reactions to skincare products, a tendency toward pigmentation, and any recent procedures -- to select a peel type and strength appropriate for you specifically. We'll also discuss your goal clearly, since "overall glow" and "targeted pigmentation improvement" call for different approaches.

## Treatment Approach

Superficial peels are well suited to general skin maintenance and mild texture concerns, with little to no visible downtime, making them easy to fit into a regular skincare routine. Medium-depth peels address more specific concerns like pigmentation and texture more directly, but require a short recovery period and more careful spacing between sessions to avoid over-treating the skin.

Meaningful, visible improvement typically comes from a planned course of several sessions rather than a single treatment, and we'll lay out a realistic course plan at your consultation rather than suggesting a single peel will resolve an established concern.

## Setting Realistic Expectations

Chemical peels improve skin texture, tone, and clarity progressively over a course of treatment -- they're a genuinely effective tool for many concerns, but results build gradually and depend on consistent skincare between sessions, not just the peels themselves.
