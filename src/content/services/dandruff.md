Dandruff -- flaking and sometimes itching of the scalp -- is common, but "dandruff" as a symptom can have several different underlying causes, from simple dry scalp to seborrheic dermatitis (an inflammatory skin condition affecting oil-producing areas) to fungal overgrowth (Malassezia yeast, which plays a role in most dandruff) to, occasionally, scalp psoriasis. Each responds best to a different treatment approach, which is why persistent dandruff that doesn't respond to standard anti-dandruff shampoos is worth a proper evaluation rather than repeatedly trying different over-the-counter products.

Most dandruff is not a sign of poor hygiene, and using anti-dandruff shampoo more often than recommended is often unhelpful and can be irritating -- a common misconception that leads people to overtreat and end up with a worse, drier scalp.

## What to Expect at Your Visit

We examine the scalp to characterize the type and distribution of flaking, checking for the redness and greasy scale typical of seborrheic dermatitis, the tight, silvery scale of psoriasis, or signs of a fungal component. We'll also review what products you've already tried and how you've been using them, since incorrect usage of otherwise appropriate anti-dandruff shampoo is a common reason treatment appears not to be working.

## Treatment Approach

For most dandruff driven by Malassezia yeast overgrowth, medicated shampoos containing specific active ingredients are effective when used correctly -- at the right frequency, left on the scalp for the recommended contact time, and used consistently rather than only when flaking is visibly bad.

Where seborrheic dermatitis is the underlying driver, targeted topical anti-inflammatory treatment addresses the condition more directly than shampoo alone. Where scalp psoriasis is present, either alongside or instead of typical dandruff, treatment shifts to address that condition specifically, since standard anti-dandruff approaches are less effective for psoriasis.

## Why "The Shampoo Isn't Working" Usually Has an Explanation

When dandruff persists despite trying multiple shampoos, the most common explanations are an incorrect underlying diagnosis, incorrect usage (not leaving the product on long enough, or not using it frequently enough), or a coexisting condition that needs separate treatment. Identifying which of these applies to you is usually enough to finally get dandruff under lasting control.
