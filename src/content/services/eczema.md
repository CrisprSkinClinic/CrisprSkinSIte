Eczema, more broadly referred to as dermatitis, describes a group of conditions causing dry, itchy, inflamed skin -- including atopic dermatitis (often linked to a personal or family history of allergies, asthma, or hay fever), contact dermatitis (triggered by a specific irritant or allergen), and other related patterns. Because the underlying trigger differs across these types, identifying which one you're dealing with shapes both immediate treatment and, more importantly, long-term prevention.

Eczema tends to be a condition of flares and remission rather than a single episode -- most patients do best with a plan that both controls active flares and reduces how often they happen, rather than treatment aimed only at the current outbreak.

## What to Expect at Your Visit

We examine the pattern and distribution of your eczema, which itself often points toward a likely cause, and take a detailed history covering personal or family history of allergic conditions, recent changes in soaps, detergents, or skincare products, occupational or environmental exposures, and weather sensitivity. Where skin appears broken or weepy, we also assess for secondary bacterial infection, which needs separate treatment.

## Treatment Approach

Active flares are treated with topical anti-inflammatory therapy at a strength and duration matched to the severity and body area involved -- under-treating a flare often just prolongs it, while over-treating unnecessarily raises other risks, so getting this calibration right matters.

Between flares, a consistent moisturizing routine using an appropriate emollient does much of the long-term preventive work, and we spend real time on this since it's often under-prioritized compared to medicated treatments. Identifying and reducing exposure to your specific triggers -- whether a product, fabric, or environmental factor -- is the other major lever for reducing how often flares occur.

## Setting Realistic Expectations

Eczema, particularly atopic dermatitis, is often a long-term pattern with periods of flare and calm rather than a condition that's cured once and never returns. Our goal is to help you understand your own triggers well enough to reduce flare frequency and severity over time, alongside effective treatment for the flares that do occur.
