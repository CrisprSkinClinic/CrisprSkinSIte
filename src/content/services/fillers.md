Dermal fillers restore volume and soften contour changes that occur with age, sun exposure, and natural facial volume loss over time -- commonly used for the cheeks, under-eye area, and lips. Unlike Botox, which relaxes muscle activity, fillers add volume directly, which makes them suited to a different set of concerns.

Our approach favors conservative, natural-looking results. The areas most prone to looking "overdone" -- lips and cheeks in particular -- are also the areas where restraint and correct technique matter most, and we'd rather under-treat on a first visit and build gradually than risk a result that doesn't look like you.

## What to Expect at Your Visit

We assess your facial structure and specific areas of volume loss or contour change, and discuss what you're hoping to achieve. Not every area or degree of change you're picturing may be the right fit for your particular facial proportions, and we'll say so directly rather than proceeding with something unlikely to look natural on you.

## Treatment Approach

Treatment is planned around your specific facial anatomy, prioritizing subtle, proportionate results. For areas like the under-eyes, where technique matters enormously and the risk of an unnatural or puffy result is higher than in other areas, we take a particularly conservative, staged approach.

Filler results are visible immediately, though some settling occurs over the following weeks. Effects are temporary and gradually diminish over months to a year or more depending on the area and product used, and many patients choose to maintain results with periodic touch-up sessions.

## Setting Realistic Expectations

Fillers restore volume and soften specific areas of contour change effectively, but they don't address every sign of facial aging -- skin quality, texture, and looseness are managed through different treatments. We discuss which of your concerns fillers can realistically address, and which would be better served by a different approach, so your treatment plan is built around your actual goals rather than a single product.
