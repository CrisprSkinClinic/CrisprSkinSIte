Laser hair reduction uses targeted light energy to reduce hair growth in treated areas over a course of sessions, working by damaging the hair follicle during its active growth phase. Because hair grows in cycles and not all follicles are in the same phase at once, a single session only affects a portion of the hair present -- which is why a planned course of multiple sessions, not a one-time treatment, is how meaningful, lasting reduction is actually achieved.

We use the term "reduction," not "removal," deliberately: laser hair reduction significantly reduces hair density and slows regrowth over a full treatment course, and many patients see long-lasting results, but it is not a guaranteed permanent removal of all hair for everyone, and occasional maintenance sessions are often needed over time.

## What to Expect at Your Visit

We assess your skin type and the color and texture of your hair, since these directly affect both how well the laser will work and which settings are safe and effective for you -- laser hair reduction works best on darker, coarser hair and is less effective on very light, fine, or grey hair regardless of provider or equipment. We'll discuss your treatment area and lay out a realistic session plan based on this assessment.

## Treatment Approach

Sessions are spaced several weeks apart, timed to catch hair follicles as they cycle into their active growth phase, since only actively growing follicles are affected by each session. Settings and pacing are adjusted for the specific treatment area, since different body regions have different hair growth cycles and skin sensitivity.

Most patients need a course of multiple sessions for substantial, visible reduction, and we lay this out clearly at your consultation rather than underselling the number of sessions actually required for a good result.

## Setting Realistic Expectations

Laser hair reduction meaningfully reduces hair growth for most patients over a full course of treatment, with regrowth -- when it occurs -- typically finer and sparser than before. We're clear that results vary by hair and skin type, that a full course (not a single session) is what produces visible change, and that periodic maintenance sessions are a normal part of sustaining results long-term for most patients.
