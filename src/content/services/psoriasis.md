Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune condition that causes skin cells to build up rapidly, forming the characteristic thick, red, scaly patches most commonly seen on the elbows, knees, scalp, and lower back, though it can affect any part of the body, including nails. It is not contagious -- a common and persistent misconception that unfortunately still affects how patients are treated socially, and one we're happy to clarify plainly for patients and family members alike.

Because psoriasis is a chronic, relapsing-remitting condition, our approach centers on long-term management rather than positioning any single treatment as a permanent cure. Most patients do well with a sustained plan that controls flares and keeps the condition manageable over years.

## What to Expect at Your Visit

We examine the extent and pattern of your psoriasis, including scalp, nails, and skin folds, which are often overlooked but relevant to treatment choice. We also ask specifically about joint pain, stiffness, or swelling, since psoriatic arthritis can accompany skin psoriasis and needs to be identified and managed separately if present.

## Treatment Approach

For limited disease affecting a small body surface area, topical therapy is usually sufficient as the primary treatment, used consistently during flares and often at a lower maintenance frequency between them. For more extensive psoriasis, phototherapy is a well-established option with a good track record, considered based on how much of the body is affected and practical access to treatment sessions.

For moderate-to-severe psoriasis, or when joints are involved, systemic treatment options are discussed and coordinated appropriately, since these decisions typically benefit from ongoing monitoring alongside treatment.

## Setting Realistic Expectations

Psoriasis is a long-term condition with a relapsing-remitting course -- flares and periods of improvement are both expected parts of managing it, not a sign that treatment has failed. We build treatment plans around sustained, long-term control instead of promising a permanent cure that current medical evidence doesn't yet support, and we help you develop a realistic sense of your own flare pattern and triggers over time.
